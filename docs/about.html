About SPC for COVID-19

The genesis of this page is described on my blog. I was trying to create a way to visualize how the infection rates were changing, and how I could tell if there was a change in on direction or another to either be worried about, or happy about.

Cindy Bush, a former colleague now working at what she called her "dream job" at CDC recalled Statistical Process Control charts to me more than a year ago as we were trying to figure out what we should be doing with telemetry data.

I spent some time figuring out how to take data from various sources that I had access to, which resulted in me spending a lot of time in Excel. A video by Henry Reich over at minutephysics on You Tube made me rethink my math just a little bit, and after a few modifications, I came to a slightly modified way of computing my formulas. Kudos also to Aatish Bhatia who created the excellent visiualization in that video.

I hate manual data entry processes, especially when they get in the way of front line health workers, so when I found an online source I decided to put it together with what I previously described as a gem of a gist on github by a fellow named Chris Rooney, a PhD specializing in visualization out of Leeds.

That's how this all started. It's up to someone else to take it further, as there is another project keeping me sane that I'm working on.
